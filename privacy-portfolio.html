Featured Project

Differentially Private Topic Modeling: Mapping Privacy Risks and Prototyping Secure Algorithms

This project explores privacy risks in topic modeling, a computational approach to analyzing large text corpora, and develops a differentially private framework for Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA). It examines how privacy-preserving mechanisms affect topic interpretability and analytical utility in topic modeling applications.

Privacy Risks in Aggregated & Anonymized Human Mobility Data for Disease Spread Modeling

Funded by the Fritz Family Fellowship and advised by Prof. Kobbi Nissim, Prof. Shweta Bansal, and Dr. Giulia Pullano, this project examines privacy risks in aggregated human mobility data used for infectious disease modelling. We show that individuals with atypical mobility patterns may remain vulnerable to membership inference despite aggregation and anonymization, and develop a differentially private framework that generates privacy-preserving county-level mobility statistics while retaining their utility for epidemiological disease spread prediction.

Navigating the Data Use & Privacy Tradeoff: An Information-Theoretic Approach to Privacy Regulation

Work advised by Prof. Kobbi Nissim, Prof. Katrina Ligett, and Dr. Ayelet Gordon-Tapiero. This project draws on information-theoretic perspectives to understand the economic properties of data use and privacy. We argue that privacy regulation should account for disclosure risks that accumulate across repeated uses of data, multiple users, data sources, and time.
