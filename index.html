Muhammad Saad

I work on questions at the intersection of technology governance, computational analysis, and data privacy. Interested in using data and technology to address public problems while preserving privacy, trust, and accountability. I focus on both technical and policy aspects, including privacy engineering and computational analysis, along with regulatory engagement and policy design.

I have have held roles at the Asia Internet Coalition, Tech & Society Institute at Georgetown, Foreign Commonwealth and Development Office, Asian Development Bank, as well as consulting for content-based platforms. I studied at Georgetown University, the National University of Singapore (NUS), and the Lahore University of Management Sciences (LUMS).
